Melanin — Nature’s Golden Biomaterial

A pigment with exceptional biological and technological potential

What is Melanin?

Melanin is a natural pigment synthesized from the amino acid tyrosine. It plays a crucial role in UV protection, has antioxidant properties, and can even conduct electricity. The three main types are eumelanin, pheomelanin, and neuromelanin. Melanin is increasingly seen as a universal functional material of the future.

Sources

Melanin can be sustainably extracted from the following biological sources:

  • 🪲 Hermetia illucens (black soldier fly)
  • 🍄 Fungi (e.g., Aspergillus niger)
  • 🧬 Human skin, hair, and eyes
  • 🧫 Microorganisms such as bacteria and algae

Hermetia larvae are a highly efficient bioresource. They can be raised on organic waste and generate high-quality melanin suitable for industrial applications.

Applications

Melanin is being explored across a range of industries:

  • 💄 Cosmetics — natural UV filters and colorants
  • 💊 Pharmaceuticals — antioxidants and radioprotectors
  • 🔬 Biomedicine — sensor coatings and nanomaterials
  • 🔌 Electronics — biocompatible organic components
